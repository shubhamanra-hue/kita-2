Why Sand Filtration Is the First Step to Clean Water

One of the most overlooked yet crucial steps in home water treatment is pre-filtration — and Sand Filter for homes provides exactly that. If your home relies on borewell water, river water, or municipal supply with high turbidity, a sand filter acts as the first line of defence. It passes water through multiple layers of graded sand, gravel, and anthracite, physically trapping sediment, silt, rust particles, and suspended matter.

The beauty of a sand filter is its simplicity — it requires no electricity to operate, is easy to maintain, and has a remarkably long life. More importantly, it protects the downstream RO membrane or UV lamp from premature damage caused by turbid water. This significantly extends the life of your primary purifier and reduces maintenance costs. Kita Systems offers premium sand filters for residential use with automatic backwash capability — ensuring continuous operation without manual intervention. If your water looks cloudy or leaves a residue in vessels, a sand filter is the first product you should invest in.

Water Ionizer for Homes — What Is It and How Does It Work?

A water ionizer for homes is an advanced kitchen appliance that uses electrolysis to restructure ordinary tap or filtered water into two streams: alkaline water for drinking and acidic water for external use. The alkaline drinking water has a pH between 8.5 and 9.5 and a negative Oxidation Reduction Potential (ORP), which means it acts as a powerful antioxidant inside your body — neutralizing harmful free radicals that accelerate aging and cause chronic disease.

Kita Systems' water ionizer machine in India features Japanese-grade titanium-platinum electrodes that are durable, food-safe, and highly effective. The machines offer multiple pH settings — alkaline levels for drinking and cooking, neutral for medication, and acidic levels for skin care and surface cleaning. A smart LCD display monitors real-time pH, ORP, and flow rate. Unlike buying bottled alkaline water (which is expensive and environmentally harmful), a home water ionizer gives your family unlimited ionized water at home — paying for itself within months. The ionized water is also micro-clustered, meaning water molecules are arranged in smaller groups that absorb into your cells 3x faster than regular water, resulting in superior hydration, better digestion, more energy, and noticeably clearer skin.

Is a water ionizer better than RO purifier for home use? +

They serve different purposes. An RO purifier removes contaminants, heavy metals, and TDS from water. A water ionizer enhances water's health properties by raising pH and creating negative ORP. How much electricity does a home water ionizer use? +

Kita Systems' water ionizers are energy-efficient and only consume electricity during actual use (while water flows through). Typical power consumption is 100–250W during operation. A water ionizer is designed to enhance water properties, not primarily to purify it. For complete purification, we recommend connecting the ionizer after an RO + UV purifier.
